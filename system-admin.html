Job description

A IT System Administrator troubleshoots technical problems for company employees and maintain the company's software operation (Coursera, 2022).

Education/Skill Advancement

  • Diploma or degree in either Computer Science or Information Technology and its related fields such as a Diploma from DMIT System Administration.
  • COMPTIA+
  • The Computing Technology Industry Association (CompTIA) is a leading voice and advocate for the global information technology ecosystem. The Website provides four different IT certifications ranging from entry-level to expert. (Kreisa, 2022)
  • Cisco Certified Network Associate (CCNA)
  • Cisco provides a certificate focusing on networking fundamentals such as security, automation, and network access. (Kreisa, 2022)

Skills required

    Technical skills

  • Experience in multiple operating systems (Windows, macOS, Linux)
  • Understanding of cyber security protocol
  • Device management
  • Experience in network management
  • Database management
  • Programming

    Soft Skills

  • Logical troubleshooting/problem solving skills
  • Adaptability to “catastrophe” (server crashes, security breaches)
  • Collaborative mindset
  • Stress management

A day in the life of the career

  • A typical workday for a System Administrator would go as follows. They start their day in the office reviewing their emails and device management suite. They will attend meetings and complete troubleshooting tickets remotely or in-person created by coworkers. In the emergency of a critical issue such as a server shutdown, the System Administrator would prioritize solving the shutdown to minimize any damage and keep up its service and, in turn, productivity. Throughout the process, they would tabulate their findings in technical documents to be discussed with differing levels of the company and investigate ways to avoid the problem or implement better systems that cause fewer problems.
